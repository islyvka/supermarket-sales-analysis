# supermarket-sales-analysis
The dataset used in this project is publicly available https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/paramvir705/supermarket-sales
# About Dataset
The growth of supermarkets in most populated cities are increasing and market competitions are also high. The dataset is one of the historical sales of supermarket company which has recorded in 3 different branches for 3 months data. Predictive data analytics methods are easy to apply with this dataset.
# Dataset includes
- Invoice ID: Computer-generated sales slip invoice identification number
- Branch: Branch of supercenter (3 branches are available identified by A, B and C)
- City: Location of supercenters 
- Customer type: Type of customers, recorded by Members for customers using member card and Normal for without member card
- Gender: Gender type of customer 
- Product line: General item categorization groups - Electronic accessories, Fashion accessories, Food and beverages, Health and beauty, Home and lifestyle, Sports and travel 
- Unit price: Price of each product in $ 
- Quantity: Number of products purchased by customer 
- Tax: 5% tax fee for customer buying 
- Total: Total price including tax 
- Date: Date of purchase (Record available from January 2019 to March 2019) 
- Time: Purchase time (10am to 9pm) 
- Payment: Payment used by customer for purchase (3 methods are available â€“ Cash, Credit card and Ewallet)
- COGS: Cost of goods sold 
- Gross margin percentage: Gross margin percentage 
- Gross income: Gross income 
- Rating: Customer stratification rating on their overall shopping experience (On a scale of 1 to 10)
# Project Overview
This project provides an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of supermarket transactions to uncover patterns in customer behavior, financial performance, and operational efficiency.

**Key Questions Answered:**

How many visits were made by customers with different customer types?
Does customer type differ by gender/ city?
How does purchasing behavior differ between Member and Normal customers?
Are there differences in purchasing behavior by gender?
What product lines are present in the dataset?
How do product lines differ in revenue, sales volume, pricing, and customer satisfaction?
Which branch is the most profitable?
How does the number of visits vary by hour of the day?
Which hours are not only busy, but also profitable?
How do sales and customer visits change during the week?

**Analytical Approach:**

1. Standardizing formats and handling date/time variables.
2. Distributing categorical and numerical features.
3. Identifying relationships between financial metrics using a Heatmap.
4. Aggregating sales by the day of the week and hours.
